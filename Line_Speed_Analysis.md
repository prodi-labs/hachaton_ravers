# Packaging Department: Line Speed & Cost Analysis (High-Nuance Model)

## 3. Simulation: The "Collaborative Block-Based" Workflow

In this environment, **Tamoa (Agent)** generates interactive blocks on the **Block Canvas** in real-time. **Tom (Manager)** interacts with these blocks to refine the plan.

**1. Tamoa (Finance):** "Tom, we need to bridge that -€500k gap. If we push the Bottler-X1 to 1,300 units/hr (+30%), my model shows we'd reduce annual runtime by 1,650 hours. At our blended labor rate of €174/hr, that's exactly €287,100 in theoretical savings—more than half our target in one move. We're only running at 50% of the design limit, so this should be feasible, right?"

> **[UI: Draft Block Generated]** — _Title: "The +30% Speed Spike". Displays a 'Savings Gauge' at €287,100 and a 'Design Limit Utilization' bar moving from 50% to 65%. The block is collapsable._

**2. Tom (Manager):** "I see the logic, Tamoa. We're at 1,000 u/h and the design limit is 2,000, so a 30% jump sounds feasible on a spreadsheet. But the reality on the floor is different. At 1,300, the labeling arm hits a resonance frequency that causes micro-vibrations. We'd see a total belt failure every two weeks, and the 'saved' labor would just be eaten up by emergency overtime. To keep the plan stable, we shouldn't cross 1,150."

> **[UI: Tom highlights "1,300 u/h" on the block]** — _A red 'Risk Warning' icon appears on the block: "Mechanical Instability: Resonance Frequency at Labeling Arm"._

**3. Tamoa (Finance):** "That actually validates my energy model, Tom. My data shows a 'hockey-stick' curve starting at 1,200 u/h—the friction heat alone would spike our cooling costs by €50k. By capping at 1,150, we stay in the high-efficiency zone. Now, let’s look at the workforce: if we hit that 1,150 mark, we reduce runtime enough to cut the freelance night-shift entirely. That’s €45/hr in 'hard' savings we can bank immediately."

> **[UI: New Draft Block Generated]** — _Title: "The 1,150 Sweet Spot". Shows a side-by-side comparison: 1,300 (High Risk) vs 1,150 (Stable). A 'Labor Mix' chart shows the freelance slice disappearing._

**4. Tom (Manager):** "Wait, Tamoa, there’s a bottleneck we're not considering: 'Bottle Drying Time'. Even if the machine runs at 1,150, the bottles coming out of the washer need a full 4 minutes to dry before the labels will stick. If we speed up the conveyor, they'll arrive at the labeling arm still damp."

> **[UI: Tom adds a 'Constraint' to the canvas]** — _Label: "4m Bottle Drying Time". The 1,150 block turns amber, indicating a bottleneck conflict._

**5. Tamoa (Finance):** "Good catch. Let me check the thermal specs on the drying tunnel... Okay, I see a variable there. To hit that 4-minute window at 1,150 u/h, we'd need to increase the tunnel temperature by 15 degrees. Can the glass handle that thermal shock?"

> **[UI: Block Animation]** — _A 'Drying Tunnel' sub-module expands. Tamoa slides the 'Temperature' toggle up by +15°C._

**6. Tom (Manager):** "Yeah, the glass is rated for it, but it’ll pull way more juice."

**7. Tamoa (Finance):** "You're right. Based on the utility rates, that temperature spike is estimated to cost €12,000 annually. It’s a small price to pay to keep the line moving, but we need to bake it into the -€500k target calculation so we don't have a surprise in Q3."

> **[UI: Financial Update]** — _The 'Savings Gauge' on the 1,150 block updates from -€180,000 to -€168,000 (reflecting the +€12k energy penalty)._

**8. Tom (Manager):** "Exactly. 1,150 is the 'Sweet Spot'. It allows my team to handle all maintenance during scheduled stops without relying on the freelance 'safety net'. It protects the machine’s life and our OEE. But to really de-risk the plan, I need those €40k sensors to catch jams before they become downtime."

> **[UI: Tom drags a 'Smart Sensor' tool into the block]** — _Tamoa calculates the ROI instantly._

**9. Tamoa (Finance):** "I can make the sensors work in the budget, Tom. Since they have a 5-year life, I'll capitalize the cost. The P&L hit this year is only €8k in depreciation, which is a fraction of the €120k in downtime labor they unlock. Before we finalize this joint plan: does 1,150 u/h risk our 'Gold Standard' seal integrity?"

> **[UI: Final Projection Block]** — _Title: "De-Risked Speed Increase Plan". Shows a timeline with 'Sensor ROI' and a 'Quality Check' green-light._

**10. Tom (Manager):** "No, that’s why 1,150 is the limit. The heat-sealer needs a specific 'dwell time' for a leak-proof bond. At 1,150, we’re right at the edge of that window. Any faster, and we’d be trading labor savings for customer returns. At 1,150, we deliver the savings _and_ the quality."

**11. Tamoa (Finance):** "Perfect. We have a robust plan: +15% speed increase + sensor-backed downtime reduction. We hit the target, protect the equipment, and keep the freelancers out of the budget. Let's lock it in."

> **[UI: Final Action]** — _Tom clicks **"Add to Initiative Review"**. The draft blocks collapse into a single, comprehensive "Production Efficiency v1.0" initiative record, stored alongside the full chat transcript._
